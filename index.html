01 // About the SRW

The Specialised Research Wing (SRW) is a compartmentalised sub-department of the Vlandask Research Corporation's Research & Studies Division. It operates on a strict need-to-know basis and conducts research that falls outside the scope and clearance of standard R&S departments.

SRW personnel are hand-selected from the R&S Division. Entry is not open to application. Personnel must hold the rank of Researcher or above and be selected by SRW Command. Upon entry, all SRW personnel are bound by this handbook and by Protocol 7-C at the SRW/L5 clearance tier.

03 // SRW Regulations
The following regulations apply to all SRW personnel at all times, in addition to standard R&S regulations. Where they conflict, SRW regulations take precedence.
>3.1 — Conduct

SRW operates under greater scrutiny than standard R&S departments. Personnel are expected to maintain the highest standard of professionalism at all times. Conduct that would result in a warning in base R&S may result in removal from SRW.

>3.2 — Confidentiality

All SRW personnel are bound by Protocol 7-C at the SRW/L5 tier. You may not disclose SRW research, assets, procedures, or personnel to anyone without explicit written authorisation from SRW Command. This obligation applies inside and outside the VRC, at all times.

>3.3 — Testing
  1. All SRW tests must be approved by a Supervisory Specialist or above before beginning.
  2. Tests must not exceed 30 minutes from rally to subject termination or return to containment.
  3. Unit 4 assets may only be tested with SRW Command present or with explicit written authorisation from the Professor.
  4. All SRW tests must be logged in the SRW documentation channel within 24 hours.
  5. Security escort requirements follow standard R&S rules scaled to the asset's risk classification.
  6. Non-cooperative or breached subjects may be ordered for termination by the hosting researcher.
>3.4 — Chain of Command
Professor → Assistant Professor → Supervisory Specialist → Assistant Supervisory Specialist → Senior Specialist → Specialist Researcher → Junior Specialist Researcher → Specialist Associate

Do not bypass the chain of command. All concerns, requests, and reports must be directed through your immediate superior first.

>3.5 — Violations & Consequences
MinorFormal warning from SRW Command. Logged on personnel record.
ModerateSuspension from SRW operations pending review by the Professor.
MajorRemoval from SRW. Possible full removal from Research & Studies.
Protocol 7-CImmediate review by Scientific Board and Executive Command. Permanent removal from VRC possible.
04 // Testing Protocols
>4.1 — Pre-Experiment Requirements
  • Written approval from a Supervisory Specialist or above.
  • Appropriate security escort confirmed and briefed before rally.
  • Documented hypothesis filed before the test begins.
  • SRW documentation channel log opened prior to start.
  • Unit 4 assets: SRW Command present or written authorisation from Professor on file.
>4.2 — During Experiments
  • Notes must be taken in real time throughout the experiment.
  • Security escort maintains containment perimeter at all times.
  • Unexpected asset behaviour must be reported over radio immediately.
  • If containment is compromised, Protocol 1-A activates — all non-combative personnel evacuate.
  • Researcher holds authority over the security escort but may not order guards to violate their own guidelines.
>4.3 — Post-Experiment Requirements
  • Full experiment log filed in the SRW documentation channel within 24 hours.
  • Any casualties or injuries reported to SRW Command and the Director of Research.
  • Asset returned to designated containment zone and confirmed secured.
  • Personnel do not disperse until asset is confirmed secure.
A missing or incomplete log is a regulation violation and will be escalated to SRW Command regardless of experiment outcome.

06 // Emergency Protocols
>Protocol 1-A — Containment Breach

Activated the moment an entity exits its designated containment zone without authorisation.

  1. Any personnel who witnesses a breach declares it immediately over radio to the highest-ranking researcher present.
  2. All non-combative personnel evacuate to the nearest designated safe zone immediately.
  3. Ranking researcher assumes incident command and notifies security.
  4. All adjacent testing operations are suspended immediately.
  5. Breached entity is not engaged by researchers — contact is security personnel's responsibility only.
  6. Unit 4 entities are terminate-on-sight unless Scientific Board+ explicitly orders otherwise.
  7. All-clear issued only by incident commander once entity is confirmed recaptured or terminated.
  8. Full incident report filed within 24 hours of the breach.
>Protocol 4-D — Biological Containment

Activated on radiation spread, infected personnel contact, or unknown biological release.

  1. Affected area sealed immediately. Non-immune personnel must evacuate before sealing.
  2. Ranking researcher notified and assumes quarantine command.
  3. No non-immune personnel may enter the quarantine zone without explicit authorisation.
  4. Immune combatives deployed to manage infected subjects within the quarantine zone.
  5. Infected subjects that cannot be controlled are terminated by immune combatives to prevent spread.
  6. Quarantine cleared only by ranking researcher once all subjects are contained or terminated.
>Protocol 5-A — Chain of Command During Incidents

Command authority during an incident passes down this chain until a willing and available individual is reached:

  1. Director of Research
  2. Assistant Director / Chief Research Officer
  3. Scientific Board (most senior present)
  4. Advisory Board (most senior present)
  5. Department Head (most senior present)
  6. Lead Researcher (most senior present)
  7. Senior Researcher (most senior present)
>Protocol 9-A — Facility Lockdown
Level 1 — PrecautionaryPotential threat identified but not confirmed. Move to nearest safe position. Await instruction. Non-essential movement discouraged.
Level 2 — Active ThreatConfirmed threat present. All non-combatives to safe zones immediately. All testing ceases. Security deploys to contain the threat.
Level 3 — CriticalSevere or uncontrolled threat. Evacuate to deepest safe zone. Only Executive Command and Scientific Board may issue or modify orders.
Lockdown may only be lifted by the individual who declared it or a higher-ranking officer. Personnel must not resume normal operations until all-clear is confirmed over radio.
07 // Protocol 7-C — Classified Information Security
This section is mandatory reading for all SRW personnel. Protocol 7-C violations are treated as the most serious class of conduct breach within the VRC.
>7.1 — SRW Classification Tier

All SRW records are classified at the L5/SRW tier — the highest available. They are accessible only to SRW members, the Scientific Board, and Executive Command. No exceptions are permitted without written authorisation from the Director of Research or Chief Research Officer.

>7.2 — Violations
  • Accessing, viewing, or distributing classified records without the required clearance.
  • Coercing, deceiving, or pressuring another personnel member into revealing classified information.
  • Sharing SRW-designated data with unauthorised individuals inside or outside the VRC.
  • Failing to report a known or suspected classified information breach.
>7.3 — Consequences

Consequences range from formal warning and rank suspension to permanent termination from the VRC, depending on severity and intent. Repeated or deliberate violations result in immediate removal without review.

>7.4 — Reporting

All suspected Protocol 7-C violations must be reported directly to the Scientific Board or Executive Command. Reports must include the nature of the breach, the personnel involved, and any available evidence. Failure to report a known violation is itself a violation of Protocol 7-C.

11 // New Member Guide
If you are reading this, you have been selected for the Specialised Research Wing. This is not a routine assignment. Read everything in this section before your first deployment.
>8.1 — What to do first
  1. Read this entire handbook. You are expected to know its contents.
  2. Acknowledge Protocol 7-C. Everything you encounter in SRW is classified.
  3. Report to your supervising Specialist Researcher or Supervisory Specialist.
  4. Do not deploy independently until instructed to do so by SRW Command.
  5. As a Specialist Associate, you may only participate under direct supervision.
>8.2 — Promotion within SRW
  • Actively participate in experiments and SRW operations.
  • Maintain a clean regulation record — violations significantly impact promotion eligibility.
  • Build a quality experiment log record in the SRW documentation channel.
  • Demonstrate understanding of SRW assets, procedures, and protocols.
  • Be formally recommended by a Supervisory Specialist or above.
Do not ask for promotions repeatedly. SRW Command observes performance continuously. When you are ready, you will be promoted.
